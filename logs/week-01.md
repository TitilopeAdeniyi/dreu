## Goals
Exams are finished. Research begins. Dr. Sun has been an amazing guide and resource even before starting this project. During the school year I was able to get a copy of the proposal and began light reading and practicing my researching skills before May. It was much needed.
Going in, I needed to figure out how I wanted to organize my files, writing, papers, and handwritten notes, and get myself used to long hours in the lab versus the long hours I spend studying for classes.
The research goal for the week was to just learn about patient trial matching. DocIds, clinical trials and realistic patient input information were needed to become comfortable with the material. 
There is a type of tone and diction needed to document patient information to be comprehended by any medical professional reading the patient info. Where as clinical trials invites a different community on the more observational side of science rather than diagnostic. Two different dialects needing to come togther to improve patient outcomes are an interesting read.

# Results
The first option is the best one. All I need are real patient cases that have been paired with multiple trials. I can make some changes to fit my project and create my own test cases with patients and trials that still exist on the site.
Real data is hard to get and verify. There are many datasets out there that could have been synthetically generated. That is fine for practice, but real patient data is raw, messy and I have no medial expertise to say otherwise. Patient data is  sometimes incoherent and always necessary that every all details are taken down. The system changing isn't the goal of my research, so my job is to adapt to it. I was never trained to pick up imposssible data, but again mistakes in data are the best tests for my project. It should be able to handle it and give insights.
